📐 Metrology

Dimensional control, GD&T, surface roughness interpretation and inspection techniques.

Overview

What is Metrology?

Metrology is the science of measurement. In manufacturing it answers one question: does this part conform to its drawing? Without reliable measurement, no process control is possible — a machine can be perfectly set up and still produce scrap if the inspection method introduces more uncertainty than the tolerance allows.

Manufacturing metrology spans from hand gages used at the machine to coordinate measuring machines (CMM) in climate-controlled labs, and increasingly to in-line optical and laser scanning systems that check every part in the production flow.

GD&T

Geometric Dimensioning & Tolerancing

GD&T (ASME Y14.5 / ISO 1101) is a symbolic language on engineering drawings that defines not only size but also the allowable variation in form, orientation, location, and runout of a feature. It replaces coordinate tolerances with functional tolerances — specifying exactly how the part must behave in its assembly.

Flatness ⏥

All points of a surface must lie between two parallel planes separated by the tolerance value.

Cylindricity ⌭

All surface points must lie in an annular zone — controls roundness, straightness, and taper simultaneously.

Perpendicularity ⊥

Feature axis or surface must be within a tolerance zone perpendicular to the datum.

Position ⊕

True position of a feature axis within a cylindrical tolerance zone. Most used GD&T symbol.

Runout ↗

Total surface variation relative to a datum axis during one full revolution.

Profile ⌓

All surface points must lie within a uniform band around the true profile. Controls complex curves.

Instruments

Measurement Tools

Vernier Caliper

0.02 mm resolution. External, internal, depth, and step measurement. Fast, shop-floor use.

Micrometer

0.001 mm resolution. Outside, inside, depth variants. Calibrated with gauge blocks.

Dial / Digital Indicator

Comparative measurement. Runout, flatness, alignment checks on surface plates.

Bore Gauge

Internal diameter measurement. Calibrate to a ring gauge or setting ring before use.

CMM

Coordinate Measuring Machine. Touch probe or scanning head. Sub-micron accuracy in controlled environment.

Profilometer

Measures surface roughness (Ra, Rz). Stylus traverses the surface; electronics compute parameters.

Surface Roughness

Ra, Rz and Finish Symbols

Ra (arithmetic mean roughness) is the average absolute deviation of the surface profile from the mean line — the most common roughness parameter. Rz (average maximum height) measures the average of the five tallest peak-to-valley heights, making it more sensitive to isolated defects like scratches.

Ra 0.4 μm → Fine turning / finish grinding
Ra 0.8 μm → Finish turning, reaming
Ra 1.6 μm → Semi-finish turning / milling
Ra 3.2 μm → General machined surfaces
Ra 6.3 μm → Rough machining, visible tool marks

Drawing surface texture symbols (ISO 1302) indicate the required Ra, the machining process allowed, and the lay direction. The machinist selects feed rate, nose radius, and cutting speed to hit the specified finish — lower feed and larger nose radius produce smoother surfaces.
